Frameworks

Structured coaching tools for deeper change work. These go beyond card prompts into guided processes that help someone map what is getting in their way and design small experiments to move through it.

Immunity to Change

Why good intentions are not enough, and what to do instead

Developed by Robert Kegan and Lisa Laskow Lahey at Harvard, the Immunity to Change framework helps people surface the hidden commitments and big assumptions that are quietly working against their stated goals. It turns frustrating stuckness into something visible and workable.

Stage 1: Name the improvement goal you keep not achieving. Stage 2: Surface the behaviours that work against it. Stage 3: Uncover the competing commitments underneath those behaviours. Stage 4: Identify the big assumptions holding the whole system in place. Stage 5: Design a small, safe-to-fail experiment to test the assumption.
What it produces
Your immunity map A clear, personalised map of the internal system keeping you stuck, plus a first experiment to gently disrupt it.
Best used for
1:1 coaching Individual sessions where someone has a clear goal but keeps hitting the same invisible wall.

How to use the library

The decks are already interactive, so this page is mainly about helping you get into the right one quickly and with the right context.

Choose by energy

Use team coaching when you need structure, Strong Suits when you want a strengths lens, and Close, Closer, Closest when the goal is relational depth.

Use on mobile too

The landing page and linked decks are set up to work as a compact card browser on smaller screens, not just as desktop workshop tools.

Mix and match

It is completely reasonable to start in one deck and switch to another once the conversation reveals what the group or person needs next.
